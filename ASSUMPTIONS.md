# ASSUMPTIONS

## About the users

The application is primarily designed for newly hired employees during their first month at Meridian.

A secondary user is the HR representative, who is responsible for maintaining onboarding content such as employees, resources, FAQs and onboarding tasks.

Managers and experienced colleagues are considered indirect users because they contribute information but do not interact with the application on a daily basis.

When opening the application for the first time, I assume that a new employee:

- has already accepted the job offer;
- has received a company laptop or has access to a personal computer;
- has internet access;
- knows only the information provided by HR before the first working day.

---

## About the data

I assume that HR is responsible for maintaining most onboarding information.

Department information changes rarely.

Employees, onboarding tasks, FAQs and learning resources are added or updated whenever necessary by HR.

If information is missing, the application still remains usable and simply displays the available data.

If incorrect information is entered, it can be corrected through the administration section.

---

## About the context

I assume that new employees mainly access the application from a desktop or laptop computer.

The application can also be used on tablets or mobile devices through its responsive layout.

I assume that employees receive access before or on their first working day so they can familiarize themselves with the company before meeting their team.
